How It Works

1

Import

Drop your video clips and music into CineBuild. Assign A-Roll (talking heads, interviews) and B-Roll (cutaway footage) with a single keypress.

2

Configure

Choose a mood, pick a style preset, set your target duration. Or just hit go — the defaults are smart.

3

The Edit Builds

Every clip is analyzed: dialogue transcribed, scenes detected, loudness measured, beats mapped, story identified. Then a complete timeline is assembled with cuts, transitions, music sync, and sound effects.

4

Preview & Refine

Watch the edit in the built-in timeline editor. Tweak anything. Then export to Final Cut Pro for finishing, or render directly.

Features

Cinematic Editing Engine

Single-Pass Editorial

Every decision a professional editor would make — clip selection, sequencing, pacing, trim points, transitions, audio placement, and narrative structure — in a single pass. No robotic patterns. CineBuild understands story tension, counterpoint, and when to hold a shot.

Narrative Structure

Story arcs are detected across your footage — hooks, introductions, rising action, climax, and resolution. Setup/payoff relationships are mapped so the edit tells a coherent story, not just a highlight reel.

Professional Cutting Patterns

Organic, varied sequencing. B-Roll montages of 3–5 clips. Strategic long holds for emotional weight. Tension-release cycles with mandatory cooldowns. The edit feels human because it's built on professional editing principles.

5 Style Presets

Music Video (fast cuts, beat-locked), Documentary (long holds, dialogue-driven), Family Memory (face-focused, warm), Action (frenetic, motion-heavy), Contemplative (slow, atmospheric). Or build your own configuration.

Documentary Mode

A-Roll / B-Roll Workflow

Assign interview and cutaway footage. All dialogue is transcribed, filler words and repeated takes detected, and the edit is built around the strongest spoken content.

Word-Level Transcription

Speech is transcribed with per-word timestamps and confidence scores. 17 filler words detected (um, uh, like, you know, basically...) with contextual awareness — distinguishing when "like" is filler vs. legitimate.

Best Take Selection

When you've recorded multiple takes of the same content, duplicates are identified and the strongest performance is selected. Or get suggestions and choose yourself.

Contextual B-Roll Matching

Relevant B-Roll is automatically placed based on what's being discussed. Five matching priorities: activity match, semantic category, keyword, emotional resonance, thematic relevance.

Smart B-Roll Placement

Four modes — concurrent (shows what's being discussed), anticipatory (shows before it's mentioned), reactive (shows after), or bridging (connects segments).

Silence & Filler Removal

Three sensitivity levels. Removes dead air and verbal tics while preserving meaningful pauses.

Music & Beat Sync

Deep Music Analysis

Upload your song and the entire structure is mapped — BPM, beat grid, song sections (intro, verse, chorus, bridge, drop, outro), vocal sections, energy curves, and key moments.

Instrument-Aware Beat Cutting

Not all beats are equal. Kick drums trigger major transitions. Snare hits drive mid-scene cuts. Cymbal crashes mark section changes. Hi-hat patterns pace rapid montages.

Music-Story Alignment

Verses map to ordinary-world footage. Choruses and drops sync to emotional peaks. Bridges match reflective moments. Outros wrap the conclusion.

Anticipatory Cuts & Strategic Silence

Cuts land slightly before the beat, matching how professional editors create the perception of sync. Music is pulled out entirely when the moment demands it — for dialogue emphasis, dramatic punctuation, or chapter transitions.

Professional Sound Design

Built-In SFX Library

11 categories of production-ready sounds — whooshes, impacts, stingers, risers, downlifters, foley (footsteps, cloth, props, body), ambience (interior, exterior, special), and transitions. UCS naming convention compliant.

Scene-Aware Sound Effects

Your SFX library is reviewed, each scene is understood, and specific sounds are placed at precise moments. Not random — intentional.

Frame-Accurate SFX Placement

DSP analysis finds the exact perceptual "hit point" in every sound file. A pre-roll formula ensures impacts land on the frame, risers peak at the moment, and ambient beds fade in naturally.

Tension Sequencing

Sub-bass drones 4–8 seconds before a hit, risers sweeping up over 6–16 seconds, a brief silence before impact, then the stinger. Cinematic sound design, automated.

Broadcast-Standard Mixing

Dialogue at -12dB, music at -14dB solo / -28dB under dialogue, SFX layered at category-appropriate levels. ITU-R BS.1770-4 loudness measurement on every clip with 0.5-second temporal resolution.

Volume Automation

5 curve types (linear, S-curve, parabolic, step, logarithmic). Music ducks for speech automatically. Every clip's actual loudness profile informs the mix.

Technical Power

Broadcast-Standard Loudness

ITU-R BS.1770-4 loudness measurement with K-weighting. Integrated LUFS, true peak, loudness range, and temporal contours for source-level-aware mixing decisions.

LUT & Camera Support

Apply .cube LUTs for color grading during preview and export. Auto-mapping for Sony camera profiles (S-Log3, S-Gamut3.Cine). Parses XML sidecar files for metadata.

Final Cut Pro Export

Full FCPXML export with storylines, connected clips, and magnetic timeline features. The edit round-trips to FCP for professional finishing.

Native macOS

Built with SwiftUI and AVFoundation. Metal GPU acceleration for preview. No FFmpeg, no Electron, no compromises. Designed for Apple Silicon.

Intelligent Caching

Previously analyzed clips are cached. Re-edit the same project with different settings without re-uploading or re-analyzing.

Learns Your Style

Per-Skill Feedback System

Rate each skill's output (transcription, scene analysis, narrative structure, etc.) on a 0–100 scale with notes. The system refines its processing instructions based on your feedback.

User Rating Priority

Star-rate your favorite clips before the edit. 4–5 star clips get a 3x selection score multiplier. 5-star clips receive special SFX treatment.

15 Modular Processing Skills

The editorial pipeline is built from 15 independent skills (transcription, B-Roll selection, narrative assembly, mood analysis, SFX suggestions, timeline generation, and more) organized in a dependency graph. Each can be refined independently.
